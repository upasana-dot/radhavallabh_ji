About the Temple

The Radha Vallabh Temple in Vrindavan, established around 1585 CE by Goswami Hit Harivansh Mahaprabhu, is a 16th-century shrine representing "Ras-bhakti" (devotion through love).

Upcoming Events

  • Khichdi Utsav (Jan/Feb 2026): A winter festival where special preparations are offered to the deity.
  • Holi Festival (March 2026): Celebrated with intense devotion, including Gulab Dol.
  • Phool Bungalow (Summer months): Floral decorations to keep the deity cool, a prominent attraction.
  • Hitotsav: An 11-day festival celebrating the founder, Hith Harivansh Mahaprabhu, featuring 'Dadhi Kando' (saffron/sandalwood curd).
  • Radhashtami: A 9-day celebration for the birth of Radha Rani, featuring grand decorations and kirtans.
  • Patotsava (November): Commemorates the day the idol was enshrined in Vrindavan.
